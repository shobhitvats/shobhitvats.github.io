Beyond the Lab

I've always believed that a researcher is more than their data, and an engineer is more than their code. My life is a constant dialogue between the analytical rigor of science and the intuitive harmony of music and art.

The Musical Journey

Music is my primary language for expression. As a multi-instrumentalist—fluent in Flute, Guitar, and Keyboard—I find that the discipline required for musical mastery often mirrors the patience needed for scientific research.

Whether it's the mathematical precision of a raga on the flute or the layered harmonies of a keyboard composition, music provides the creative reset that fuels my analytical breakthroughs.

🪈 🎸 🎹

"Where words fail, music speaks."

Digital Art & Design

My interest in Digital Art and Visual Design isn't just a hobby—it's a critical tool for my scientific work. Being able to visualize complex molecular interactions or design intuitive user interfaces stems from a deep appreciation for aesthetics and composition.

I've applied this throughout my time at IISER, from leading institutional design teams to creating the visual identity for flagship festivals.

🎨 ✍️ 🖥️

"Design is intelligence made visible."
